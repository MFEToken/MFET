What is MFEToken contract and how does it work?

I coded an ERC20 SmartContract called MyFirstEthereumToken and registered on to the Ethereum Network, you can see the code on my GitHub and post any questions on MFEToken Twitter account.

The contract is very simple, it contains and owner (Me) basic methods to transfer and make it compatible with any other contract according to the ERC20 standard and a default payable method that allows you to buy MFET by just sending Ether into the Smart Contract Address.

The formula to get tokens its very simple, you get 1000 MFET for every ether you transfer, as a bonus you can get 10 times “x” the total number of Ethereums that have been collected as a percentage multiplier.
(Your Contribution * 1000) + (Your Contribution * 0.1 * Acumulated Contribution) = Total MFET

You can easily understand with the following examples:

Example 1

Let’s say there are 0 Ether contributed (This will happen at the beginning and I hope only the beginning) and you contribute 0.5 Ether into the Smart Contract Address, you will then receive 500 MFET + 10 times 0 as a % tokens. Meaning you get only 500 MFET.

(0.5 * 1000) + (10 * 0 * (0.5 * 1000)) = 500 MFET

500 MFET + 0 MFET = 500 MFET

Example 2

On the other hand, lets assume there are 3 Ether contributed (The sum of everyone’s contribution at the time of contribution) and you decide to transfer 0.5 Ether. In this case you will receive 500 MFET + (10 * 3) 30% of your contribution as additional tokens

(0.5 * 1000) + (0.1 * 3 * (0.5 * 1000)) = 500 MFET

500 MFET + 150 MFET = 650 MFET

Example 3

On the other hand, lets assume there are 12 Ether contributed and you decide to transfer 0.5 Ether. In this case you will receive 500 MFET + (10 * 12) 120% of your contribution as additional tokens

(0.5 * 1000) + (0.1 * 12 * (0.5 * 1000)) = 1100 MFET

500 MFET + 600 MFET = 1100 MFET
